Artizan Design Hotel

Georgia, Tbilisi
Overview Rooms Reviews Location

Stylish Rooms and a Historic Artist's Museum in Tbilisi's Vera District

Artizan Design Hotel sits in the heart of Vera, shaped by a quiet reverence for Georgian craft and the legacy of sculptor Iakob Nikoladze. At the center of the property is the historic Iakob Nikoladze House-Museum — his former home and studio, preserved with original works and intimate details that allow guests to step briefly into the artist's world. A short guided visit, easily arranged at reception, reveals why this creative landmark remains central to the hotel's identity.

Inside, the atmosphere is warm, calm, and pared back. Rooms are designed with natural textures, soft lighting, and clean, contemporary lines that feel both intentional and easy to settle into. Some look onto the peaceful courtyard, others toward the surrounding neighborhood, but all carry the same understated aesthetic that runs through the hotel.

In the courtyard, Iakobis Ezo brings together food, sound, and a local crowd, offering straightforward plates, natural wines, and occasional DJ sets. With thoughtful interiors, a cultural heritage site on the grounds, and Vera's cafes, galleries, and small restaurants just outside the door, Artizan blends boutique comfort with the creative rhythm of the neighborhood.

Location

Artizan Design Hotel sits in Vera, one of Tbilisi's most atmospheric central neighborhoods, known for its tree-lined streets, small galleries, vintage shops, and an easy flow of cafes, wine bars, and local hangouts. The area blends creative energy with a lived-in, residential calm, making it an ideal base for exploring the city on foot. Rustaveli Avenue, with its theaters and museums, is close by, while the surrounding streets offer the kind of everyday rhythm that gives Vera its character.
