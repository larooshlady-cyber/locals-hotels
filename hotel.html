Stylish Rooms and a Historic Artist's Museum in Tbilisi's Vera District

Nestled in the heart of Tbilisi's historic Old Town, Artizan Design Hotel is a meticulously restored 19th-century building that bridges Georgian heritage with contemporary design. Each of the 37 rooms is individually appointed with locally sourced furnishings, original artwork, and artisan-crafted details that pay homage to the city's vibrant creative scene. The rooftop terrace offers sweeping views of Narikala Fortress and the Mtkvari River, while the ground-floor restaurant serves modern Georgian cuisine using ingredients from the hotel's own partnerships with Kakhetian winemakers and local foragers.

Location

Artizan Design Hotel is set on Kote Apkhazi Street, one of the most historic streets in Tbilisi. Once part of the city's main merchant route, it now carries layers of daily life, shared sidewalks, shop windows, church bells, and old keys still turning in iron doors. The hotel sits near the midpoint, where foot traffic thickens and side alleys lead toward hidden wine cellars, antique stores, and inner courtyards that still hold the city's older rhythm.

From the entrance, it's a short walk to Meidan, the sulfur baths, or the open-air cafés that lean into the curve of the old town. Whether walking downhill toward the river or uphill toward Sololaki, the location holds you close to everything.
